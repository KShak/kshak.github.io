Yosemite Summer 2015

I had the pleasure of visiting Yosemite twice during the summer. Both times I hiked The Mist Trail (Vernal and Nevada Falls). Unfortunately, I was focused more on the hikes than on taking pictures, but I did get a few and they summarize the scenery fairly well. Since the first trip was a bit rushed and more of a discovery experience, I was not able to swim in any of the pools above the waterfalls, but I wasted no time during the second trip getting to these pools. The water was cold and refreshing, especially since it was about 90 degrees Fahrenheit. I look forward to many more trips to Yosemite and I highly recommend a visit if you have not been.

Bulgaria Winter 2014-15

I have wanted to visit Europe since I started taking French classes in early middle school. My first trip was not to France, but rather to eastern Europe, mainly Bulgaria. The only bad experience was the fourteen-hour journeys which were surprisingly comfortable; I just dislike confined spaces with little to do. I now consider Bulgaria my second home as the people, places and food are absolutely amazing. Within Bulgaria, I was able to explore Sofia and Plovdiv, two amazing cities with distinct and ancient cultures. Outside of Bulgaria, I was also able to travel to Niche in Serbia and Thessaloniki and Olympic Beach in Greece. What made me feel the most at home and welcomed aside from the people I was with was the lack of substantial tourism. The experience I was getting felt and was natural. I plan on returning to Bulgaria and exploring more of Europe in Winter 2015.

Silverton Colorado Summer 2013

Took a spontaneous trip with some friends the summer before starting college. Silverton is a small, old mining town. 9,318 feet above sea level. Stunning landscapes. Good people.
